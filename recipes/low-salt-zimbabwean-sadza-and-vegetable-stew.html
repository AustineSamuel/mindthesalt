Low-Salt Zimbabwean Sadza and Vegetable Stew Recipe



Low-Salt Zimbabwean Sadza and Vegetable Stew

Zimbabwean Sadza and Vegetable Stew is a traditional meal combining a thick maize porridge with a hearty vegetable stew. This version reduces salt while keeping the flavours intact.

Ingredients

For Sadza (Maize Porridge):

  • 2 cups maize meal - the main ingredient.
  • 4 cups water - for cooking.
  • Pinch of salt (optional) - adjust sparingly or omit completely.

For Vegetable Stew:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil - for cooking.
  • 1 onion, finely chopped - adds sweetness and flavour.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced - enhances flavour.
  • 2 carrots, diced - adds sweetness and texture.
  • 2 potatoes, diced - adds substance.
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped - adds acidity and moisture.
  • 2 cups chopped leafy greens (such as spinach or kale) - adds greens.
  • 1 cup cooked beans (such as kidney beans or black-eyed peas) - provides protein.
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin - adds earthy warmth.
  • 1 teaspoon paprika - for colour and mild heat.
  • Freshly ground black pepper - for added flavour.
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped - for garnish.

Instructions

For Sadza (Maize Porridge):

  1. Boil Water: In a large pot, bring water to a boil over high heat.
  2. Add Maize Meal: Gradually add maize meal to the boiling water, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming.
  3. Cook and Stir: Reduce the heat to low and continue stirring until the mixture thickens and the maize meal is fully cooked, about 20-30 minutes.
  4. Season (Optional): Season with a pinch of salt if desired. Use salt sparingly or omit completely.

For Vegetable Stew:

  1. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot or skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add finely chopped onion and sauté until softened and translucent.
  2. Add Vegetables: Stir in minced garlic, diced carrots, and potatoes. Cook until slightly softened.
  3. Combine Ingredients: Add chopped tomatoes, leafy greens, and cooked beans. Mix well with the other ingredients.
  4. Season: Season with ground cumin, paprika, and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  5. Simmer: Cover the pot and let the stew simmer gently for about 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the flavours have melded together.

To Serve:

  1. Serve the sadza alongside the vegetable stew, spooning the stew over the porridge.
  2. Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves for added freshness and flavour.
