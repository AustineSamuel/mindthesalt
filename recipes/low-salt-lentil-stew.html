Low-Salt Ethiopian Lentil Stew (Misir Wot) Recipe



Low-Salt Ethiopian Lentil Stew

Misir Wot is a flavourful and spicy Ethiopian lentil stew. This low-salt version retains the authentic taste of the dish while being mindful of sodium intake.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed and drained - the main ingredient.
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil - for cooking.
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped - adds sweetness and depth.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced - enhances flavour.
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger - adds warmth.
  • 1 tablespoon berbere spice blend - provides authentic Ethiopian flavour (adjust to taste).
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin - adds earthy warmth.
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric - for colour and its anti-inflammatory properties.
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika - for colour and a mild heat.
  • 2 cups low-sodium vegetable broth - for cooking.
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes - forms the base of the stew.
  • Salt to taste (optional) - adjust sparingly or omit completely.
  • Freshly ground black pepper - for added flavour.
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped - for garnish.

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add chopped onion and sauté until softened and translucent. Stir in minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the Spices: Sprinkle berbere spice blend, ground cumin, ground turmeric, and paprika over the onion mixture. Stir well to coat the onions with the spices.
  3. Cook the Lentils: Add rinsed red lentils to the pot, stirring to combine with the onion and spice mixture. Pour in low-sodium vegetable broth and diced tomatoes. Stir well to incorporate all ingredients.
  4. Simmer the Stew: Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the stew simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the lentils are tender and the stew has thickened.
  5. Adjust Seasoning and Serve: Taste the Ethiopian Lentil Stew and adjust seasoning with salt and freshly ground black pepper if necessary. Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves before serving. Serve hot with injera or rice.
