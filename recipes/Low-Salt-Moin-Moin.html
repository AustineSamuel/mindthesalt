Low-Salt Moin Moin Recipe

Low-Salt Moin Moin

Moin Moin is a flavourful Nigerian steamed bean pudding that is traditionally rich in proteins and versatile in serving options. This version retains its delightful flavours while being mindful of sodium intake.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups black-eyed peas, soaked overnight and skins removed - the main ingredient.
  • 1 large onion, chopped - adds a base flavour.
  • 1 red bell pepper, deseeded and chopped - contributes sweetness and colour.
  • 1 green bell pepper, deseeded and chopped - adds freshness and contrast.
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper (optional), deseeded and chopped - for authentic heat; adjust to taste.
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced - enhances depth.
  • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish (optional) - adds a unique flavour, commonly used in Nigerian cooking.
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric - for colour and its anti-inflammatory properties.
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin - gives a warm, earthy note.
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil - for moisture and richness.
  • Water - enough to blend the mixture to a pourable consistency.
  • Banana leaves or foil - for wrapping the pudding.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Bean Mixture: Drain the soaked black-eyed peas and rub them between your hands or in a towel to remove as much of the skins as possible. In a blender, combine the skinned black-eyed peas, onion, red and green bell peppers, Scotch bonnet pepper, and garlic. Add just enough water to facilitate blending. Blend until smooth.
  2. Mix the Seasonings: Transfer the blended mixture to a large bowl. Stir in the ground crayfish (if using), turmeric, cumin, and olive oil. Mix thoroughly to ensure even distribution of spices.
  3. Wrap the Pudding: Prepare your steaming vessel by bringing water to a simmer. If using banana leaves, scald them briefly in hot water to make them pliable. Cut into rectangles large enough to hold a few tablespoons of the bean mixture. Alternatively, prepare squares of aluminum foil. Spoon the bean mixture into the leaves or foil and fold securely, ensuring the mixture is well contained.
  4. Steam the Moin Moin: Place the wrapped puddings in a steamer basket or on a rack above the simmering water in a covered pot. Steam for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the Moin Moin feels firm and has solidified. The cooking time may vary depending on the size of the puddings.
  5. Serve: Allow the Moin Moin to cool slightly before unwrapping. It can be served warm or at room temperature. Moin Moin is versatile and can be enjoyed on its own, with a side of rice, or as part of a larger meal.
