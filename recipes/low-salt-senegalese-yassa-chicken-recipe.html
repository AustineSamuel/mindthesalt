Low-Salt Senegalese Yassa Chicken Recipe

Low-Salt Senegalese Yassa Chicken

Senegalese Yassa Chicken is a tangy and flavourful dish featuring marinated chicken cooked with onions, lemon, and mustard. This version is crafted to be low in salt while maintaining its traditional flavours.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs - provides protein.
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced - the base of the dish.
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced - enhances flavour.
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger - adds warmth.
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil - for cooking.
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard - adds tanginess.
  • Juice of 2 lemons - adds acidity.
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper - for added flavour.
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika - for colour and mild heat.
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (optional) - for extra heat.
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth - for cooking.
  • Salt to taste (optional) - adjust sparingly or omit completely.
  • Fresh parsley or coriander, chopped - for garnish.
  • Cooked rice or couscous, for serving.

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine minced garlic, grated ginger, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, ground black pepper, ground paprika, and ground cayenne pepper (if using). Mix well to form a marinade. Add the chicken thighs, ensuring they are evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.
  2. Sauté the Onions: Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or pan over medium heat. Add thinly sliced onions and sauté until softened and lightly browned, about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Cook the Chicken: Remove the marinated chicken from the refrigerator and shake off excess marinade. Push the onions to the side of the skillet and add the chicken, skin-side down. Cook for 5-6 minutes on each side, or until browned. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.
  4. Deglaze the Pan: Pour low-sodium chicken broth into the skillet, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. This enhances the sauce flavour.
  5. Simmer the Sauce: Return the chicken to the skillet, nestling it among the onions. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes to meld the flavours.
  6. Adjust Seasoning and Serve: Taste the Yassa Chicken and adjust seasoning with salt if needed, keeping it minimal. Garnish with parsley or coriander. Serve hot with rice or couscous.
