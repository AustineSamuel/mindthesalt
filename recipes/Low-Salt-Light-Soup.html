Low-Salt Light Soup Recipe



Low-Salt Light Soup with Fufu

Fufu and Light Soup is a staple dish across West Africa. This recipe focuses on creating a flavourful soup without added salt, complementing the neutral taste of Fufu.

Ingredients for the Soup

  • 300 grams of lean chicken or fish, cut into pieces - provides protein.
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil - for sautéing.
  • 1 large onion, chopped - base flavour.
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced - adds depth.
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, minced - brings a sharp, zesty flavour.
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed - forms the soup base.
  • 1 medium carrot, chopped - adds sweetness and texture.
  • 1 bell pepper, any colour, chopped - adds sweetness and body to the soup.
  • 1 litre of water or low-sodium chicken/vegetable broth - the liquid base of the soup.
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika - for colour and a mild peppery flavour.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric - for colour and its anti-inflammatory properties.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper - enhances flavour without adding salt.
  • Fresh basil or cilantro, chopped - for garnish and freshness.

Ingredients for Fufu

  • 2 cups of cassava flour or instant fufu flour - the main ingredient for Fufu.
  • Water - for mixing the Fufu.

Instructions

  1. Make the Light Soup: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, and ginger, sautéing until onions are translucent. Add the pureed tomatoes, chopped carrots, and bell pepper. Cook until the vegetables are soft. Add the chicken or fish pieces, paprika, turmeric, and black pepper. Stir well to combine. Pour in water or low-sodium broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the meat is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  2. Prepare the Fufu: Boil water in a large pot. Gradually add the cassava or instant fufu flour while stirring vigorously to prevent lumps. Keep stirring until the mixture becomes thick and smooth. Once the fufu is cooked (it should have a sticky, dough-like consistency), turn off the heat and let it sit covered for a few minutes.
  3. Serve: Shape the fufu into balls using wet hands. Serve in a bowl alongside the hot light soup. Garnish the soup with fresh basil or cilantro before serving.
