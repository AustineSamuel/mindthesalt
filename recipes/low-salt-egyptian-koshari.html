Low-Salt Egyptian Koshari Recipe



Low-Salt Egyptian Koshari

Egyptian Koshari is a hearty and flavourful dish that combines rice, lentils, macaroni, chickpeas, and a tangy tomato sauce, topped with crispy fried onions. This version is mindful of salt content while keeping all the rich flavours intact.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried brown lentils, rinsed and drained - provides protein.
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice - for the rice base.
  • 1 cup dried macaroni - adds texture.
  • 1 cup canned chickpeas, rinsed and drained - provides protein.
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil - for cooking.
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced - for frying.
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced - enhances flavour.
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste - for the tomato sauce.
  • 2 cups crushed tomatoes - adds acidity and moisture.
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin - adds earthy warmth.
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander - adds citrusy undertones.
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon - adds warmth and depth.
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves - adds warmth and depth.
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg - adds warmth and depth.
  • Salt to taste (optional) - adjust sparingly or omit completely.
  • Freshly ground black pepper - for added flavour.
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped - for garnish.
  • Chilli flakes or hot sauce, for serving (optional).

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Lentils and Rice: Cook the brown lentils and white rice in separate pots according to package instructions until tender. Drain any excess water and set aside.
  2. Cook the Macaroni: Cook the macaroni according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. Fry the Onions: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add thinly sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the skillet and drain on paper towels.
  4. Prepare the Tomato Sauce: In the same skillet, add minced garlic and saut√© until fragrant. Stir in tomato paste and cook for a minute to remove any raw taste. Add crushed tomatoes, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground cinnamon, ground cloves, and ground nutmeg. Stir well to combine. Let the sauce simmer for about 10-15 minutes, until thickened. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  5. Assemble the Koshari: In individual serving bowls or plates, layer cooked rice, lentils, macaroni, and chickpeas. Spoon the tomato sauce over the top of each serving. Garnish with crispy fried onions and chopped fresh coriander leaves.
  6. Serve: Serve hot, with chilli flakes or hot sauce on the side for those who prefer extra heat.
